## Part 1: ELD Integrity and the End of Tampering Leniency

The most aggressive change in the 2026 criteria involves the **Electronic Logging Device (ELD)**. As of April 1, 2026, the CVSA has removed the grey area surrounding device integrity under **49 CFR §395.8**.

### 1.1 ELD Tampering as an Immediate OOS Violation
Under the new criteria, if a roadside inspector detects any evidence of **ELD tampering**—including the use of GPS signal jammers, unauthorized software "ghost" logs, or hardware bypasses—the vehicle and driver will be placed Out-of-Service immediately.
* **The "Unable to Reconstruct" Rule:** If tampering makes it impossible to identify duty status events, an OOS condition now applies. The inspector is no longer required to allow the driver to revert to paper logs if the records cannot be verified.
* **Why it matters:** This shift moves ELD violations from simple record-keeping errors to "Safety Device Failures," carrying maximum severity weights in the CSA scoring system.

## Part 2: The 0.5% Alcohol Threshold and Zero Tolerance Possession

The CVSA has clarified the thresholds for alcohol possession under **49 CFR §392.5**, aligning inspection criteria with strict federal definitions.

### 2.1 The "Non-Alcoholic" Fallacy
A major point of contention has been the possession of "non-alcoholic" beverages that contain trace amounts of alcohol. 
* **The 2026 Standard:** Any beverage found in the cab containing **0.5% or more alcohol by volume** (including certain kombuchas or "non-alcoholic" beers) is now a mandatory OOS violation. 
* **Possession Rules:** The presence of any distilled spirits or alcoholic beverages in the passenger area or sleeper berth results in an immediate **24-hour OOS order** for the driver.

## Part 3: Brake System Integrity – The 1-Inch Chafing Standard

Mechanical failures remain the leading cause of vehicle-based OOS orders. The 2026 update (denoted with an asterisk in the handbook) provides a definitive measurement for **Brake Hose Chafing**.

### 3.1 The 1-Inch Threshold
Previously subjective, the new measurement guidance is clear:
* **The Rule:** Any brake hose chafing that exposes the reinforcement material over a length **greater than 1 inch (2.54 cm)** is now an automatic Out-of-Service condition.
* **Consistency:** This rule removes "inspector intuition" and requires a physical measurement. If the reinforcement ply is visible and frayed over a 1-inch span, the truck is sidelined.

## Part 4: CDL Endorsements and Restrictions (§383.93 & §383.95)

The 2026 Green Paper emphasizes **License Integrity**, particularly for drivers operating specialized equipment.

### 4.1 Restriction "E" and "L" Enforcement
* **Manual vs. Automatic (Restriction E):** Drivers with an "E" restriction found operating a manual transmission CMV will face a primary OOS violation. 
* **Air Brakes (Restriction L):** Operating an air brake-equipped CMV without the proper endorsement or with an "L" restriction is now a "No-Go" violation at the roadside.
* **Intrastate "K" Restriction:** CVSA clarified that the "K" restriction prohibits operating in *interstate* commerce, but does not prevent intrastate portions of interstate movements—a vital distinction for routing.
